Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 27, 2026

Short version: Tally does not collect, transmit, or share any of your data. Everything happens on your device. There are no accounts, no analytics, and no servers.

What Tally does on your device

Tally is a video editor for dance practice and performance footage. All of its features — dancer tracking, face blurring, audio sync, and visual effects — run entirely on-device using local machine learning models. Nothing about your videos, your edits, or your usage of the app is ever sent off your phone.

What we access, and why

  • Photo library — Tally asks for access to import videos you choose, and to save your edited videos back to your camera roll when you export. Tally never reads or accesses any photo or video you don't explicitly select.
  • Microphone — used only if you choose to record audio directly in the app (for example, to sync against a studio track). Audio is processed and stored locally.

What we store

All projects, edits, and exported videos are stored locally in the app's private storage on your device. Tally has no backend server, no cloud sync, and no user accounts — there is nothing tied to your identity for us to store in the first place.

Third parties

Tally does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting services. The on-device machine learning used for dancer tracking runs locally and does not transmit data anywhere.

Children's privacy

Tally does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children, because it does not collect information at all.

Changes to this policy

If Tally's data practices ever change, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will reflect that.
